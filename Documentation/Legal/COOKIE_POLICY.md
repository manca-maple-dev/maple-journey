This Cookie Policy explains how MapleJourney uses cookies and similar technologies on our website, application, and related services.

## 1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website or use an application. Similar technologies may include local storage, pixels, tags, SDKs, and device identifiers.

## 2. Why We Use Cookies and Similar Technologies

We may use these technologies to:

- keep you signed in
- remember settings and preferences
- maintain session security
- detect abuse and protect accounts
- understand usage and improve the Services
- measure performance and reliability
- support communications, billing, or feature functionality

## 3. Categories of Cookies We May Use

### Strictly necessary

These are required for the Services to function, including login sessions, security tokens, fraud prevention, and load balancing.

### Functional

These remember choices such as language, preferences, onboarding state, or interface settings.

### Analytics

These help us understand traffic, engagement, errors, and feature usage.

### Performance and reliability

These help diagnose technical issues and monitor service quality.

### Marketing or advertising

We do not currently advertise that MapleJourney uses targeted advertising cookies. If that changes, this policy and any consent flows must be updated before deployment.

## 4. Browser Storage and Local Storage

MapleJourney may use local storage or similar browser storage to retain certain application state, such as onboarding progress, preferences, or temporary session data.

## 5. Managing Cookies

Most browsers let you control cookies through settings. You can usually block or delete cookies, but some Services may not function correctly if you do.

You can also adjust device or browser settings to limit tracking technologies where available.

## 6. Third-Party Technologies

Third-party vendors that support hosting, analytics, authentication, payments, communications, and other features may place or read cookies or similar technologies subject to their own policies.
